# 09 — Smoke Testing

> Phase 3B: Pre-build validation. Prove real buyer interest before investing time building the product. Every method generates conversion data AND upgrades warm leads.

---

## Smoke Testing Philosophy

| Principle | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **Test before you build** | Spending 2-4 weeks building a product nobody wants is the #1 solo founder mistake |
| **Real conversion > survey intent** | Someone clicking "Buy Now" is 10× more reliable than saying "I'd buy" |
| **Multiple validation approaches** | Different methods test different things — use 2+ for confidence |
| **Lead temperature upgrade** | Every smoke test interaction upgrades lead data (Doc 04) |
| **Speed over perfection** | A quick smoke test in 3-5 days beats a perfect one in 3 weeks |

---

## Method Selection Guide

```
What's your opportunity's strongest trait?
│
├── Urgency/deadline exists
│   └── Method C: Pre-Sale Test (urgency justifies buying sight-unseen)
│
├── Active communities discussing this pain
│   └── Method B: Concierge Test (engage directly with sufferers)
│
├── Clear product concept + enough time
│   └── Method A: Landing Page Test (cleanest conversion data)
│
└── Unsure about exact format/angle
    └── Method D: Audience Polling (clarify before committing)

Best practice: Use TWO methods for each opportunity
```

## Method A: Landing Page Test

### Setup

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Tool** | Carrd ($19/year), Systeme.io (free), or Unbounce |
| **Setup time** | 2-4 hours |
| **Run time** | 5-10 days |
| **Target visitors** | 200-500 |

### Page Structure

```
HEADLINE: [Pain-focused headline — reference their words from discovery]
SUBHEAD: [What the product is and who it's for]

PROBLEM SECTION:
  → 3-4 pain points using exact language from Phase 1 research
  → "Sound familiar?" hook

SOLUTION SECTION:
  → What the product covers (bullet points)
  → Product format(s) — PDF, templates, course, etc.
  → Key benefit: "Save $X vs. hiring a professional"
  → Price anchor: "CPAs charge $X for this. Get it for $Y."

SOCIAL PROOF:
  → "Based on analysis of [N] [niche] professionals' top complaints"
  → If available: early endorsements, expert quotes

CTA SECTION:
  → Option 1: "Get Early Access" (email capture — measures interest)
  → Option 2: "Pre-Order at Launch Price" (payment intent — strongest signal)
  → Option 3: "Join Waitlist" (email capture — moderate signal)

FOOTER:
  → Refund guarantee
  → "Questions? [email]"
```

### Traffic Sources (Ordered by Quality)

| Source | Method | Cost | Expected CTR |
|--------|--------|------|-------------|
| **Warm leads (Doc 04)** | Direct outreach to hot/warm leads | $0 | 15-30% |
| **Reddit (value post)** | Provide value in relevant subreddit, link in profile/comments | $0 | 2-5% |
| **Community posting** | Post in niche groups (with permission) | $0 | 3-8% |
| **Cold outreach** | DM/email people expressing pain (carefully, respectfully) | $0 | 1-3% |
| **Google Ads** | Small budget ($50-100) targeting "[problem] template/solution" | $50-100 | 5-15% |
| **Facebook Ads** | Small budget ($50-100) targeting niche interest audiences | $50-100 | 3-8% |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Strong Signal | Moderate | Weak |
|--------|-------------|----------|------|
| **Email opt-in rate** | > 25% of visitors | 10-25% | < 10% |
| **Pre-order/buy click** | > 3% of visitors | 1-3% | < 1% |
| **Actual payment** | > 2% of visitors | 0.5-2% | < 0.5% |
| **Waitlist signups** | > 50 in first week | 20-50 | < 20 |

## Method B: Concierge Test

### Overview

Manually deliver the solution to 3-5 people from your warm lead list, then validate demand for the productized version.

### Process

```
1. Select 3-5 warm/hot leads who expressed the strongest pain
2. Reach out: "I'm building [product] for [persona]. Would you be willing
   to try a beta version for free in exchange for feedback?"
3. Manually create a bare-bones version for their specific situation
4. Deliver it and observe: Do they use it? What questions do they ask?
5. After delivery, ask:
   - "Was this helpful?"
   - "What was missing?"
   - "Would you pay $[X] for a complete version?"
   - "Do you know others who need this?"
```

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Strong Signal | Moderate | Weak |
|--------|-------------|----------|------|
| **Acceptance rate** | 4-5 out of 5 accept beta | 2-3 out of 5 | < 2 out of 5 |
| **Actually used it** | All participants used + provided feedback | Some used, some ghosted | None actually used it |
| **WTP confirmation** | Confirmed they'd pay at target price | Would pay but less than target | Would not pay |
| **Referrals** | Offered to share with colleagues | Said "maybe" | No referral interest |

## Method C: Pre-Sale Test

### Overview

Accept real payment before the product is built. Strongest conversion signal possible.

### Setup

| Component | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Payment processor** | Stripe, Gumroad, Systeme.io |
| **Pre-sale page** | Clear product description + expected delivery date |
| **Refund policy** | "Full refund if not delivered by [date] or if you're unsatisfied" |
| **Setup time** | 2-4 hours |

### Pre-Sale Page Must Include

```
✅ Clear product description (what it covers, format, who it's for)
✅ Expected delivery date (be specific — "Shipping by [date]")
✅ Pre-sale pricing (discount vs. eventual full price)
✅ Full refund guarantee
✅ FAQ addressing common concerns
✅ Contact email for questions
```

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Strong Signal | Moderate | Weak |
|--------|-------------|----------|------|
| **Pre-orders** | 10+ in first week | 5-9 | < 5 |
| **Revenue** | > $500 in first week | $100-500 | < $100 |
| **Refund rate** | < 5% after delivery | 5-15% | > 15% (positioning problem) |

### Ethics Note

Pre-sales are ethical when:
- Product will genuinely be delivered as described
- Clear delivery date is communicated
- Easy, no-questions-asked refunds are available
- Product quality matches or exceeds what was promised

## Method D: Audience Polling

### Overview

Structured polls in communities where the target audience congregates. Lower signal strength than other methods but useful for clarifying product direction.

### Poll Design

```
POLL: [Community name]
──────────────────────

Context: "I'm researching [problem area] for [persona].
Quick poll — which would be most helpful?"

Options:
A. Step-by-step compliance guide ($97) — covers [scope]
B. Template pack ($47) — plug-and-play [templates]
C. Video walkthrough ($197) — watch me do [process]
D. Calculator/tool ($19/mo) — automated [function]
E. None — I already have this covered

Follow-up comment: "For anyone who voted, what's your biggest frustration
with [existing solutions / current approach]?"
```

### Distribution Channels

| Channel | Method |
|---------|--------|
| Reddit | Post poll in relevant subreddit (check rules first) |
| LinkedIn | Article + poll in professional groups |
| Facebook Groups | Post in niche groups (with admin permission) |
| Twitter/X | Thread + poll |
| Email list | If you have one — send to warm leads |

### Success Metrics

| Metric | Strong Signal | Moderate | Weak |
|--------|-------------|----------|------|
| **Poll responses** | 50+ votes | 20-50 | < 20 |
| **Clear winner** | One option > 40% | Leading option 25-40% | No clear winner |
| **Follow-up engagement** | 10+ comments with details | 5-10 comments | < 5 |

## Lead Temperature Upgrades During Smoke Testing

Every smoke test interaction is a lead temperature event:

| Interaction | Temperature Before → After | Record Update |
|-------------|---------------------------|---------------|
| Visited landing page | N/A (anonymous unless known lead) | Track if from warm lead outreach link |
| Signed up for waitlist | Cold → Hot | `conversion_type` = "waitlist_signup", capture email |
| Pre-ordered product | Cold/Warm → Hot | `conversion_type` = "pre_order", capture email + payment |
| Accepted beta test | Warm → Hot | `conversion_type` = "beta_tester" |
| Used beta + gave feedback | Hot → Hot (enriched) | Add `response`, `response_sentiment` |
| Voted in poll | Cold → Warm (if identifiable) | Track engagement |
| Referred others | Any → upgrade + flag as "referrer" | High-value lead — potential affiliate |
| Declined / no response | No change | Note in record |

## Pivot vs. Kill Decision

After smoke test results:

```
Results strong (hit "Strong Signal" thresholds)?
├── Yes → ADVANCE to Blueprint (Phase 4)
└── No →
    Were specific objections identified?
    ├── Yes, fixable (price too high, wrong format, missing feature)
    │   └── PIVOT — adjust product and re-test (max 1 pivot)
    ├── Yes, fundamental (don't need it, already solved, won't pay)
    │   └── KILL — demand not validated
    └── No clear feedback, just low conversion
        └── Check traffic quality. If traffic was right audience and low conversion → KILL
            If traffic was wrong audience → retest with better targeting (1 attempt)
```
